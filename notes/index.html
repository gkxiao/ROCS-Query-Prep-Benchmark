Conclusions

The evidence demonstrates unequivocally that query preparation has a profound impact on ROCS-based virtual screening performance. Traditional minimum energy approaches are consistently outperformed by sophisticated strategies considering conformational ensembles, feature decomposition, scaffold focusing, or multiple references.

The CORAL methodology provides systematic conformational optimization that should be considered essential for virtual screening workflows. Feature decomposition enables system-specific optimization, while scaffold-focused methods excel at identifying structurally novel compounds.

The multi-reference approach represents the current state-of-the-art, offering comprehensive molecular characteristic representation. However, the target-dependent nature of optimal strategies emphasizes the need for careful evaluation and selection.

Key Takeaway for Practitioners:

Query preparation should be considered a critical component of virtual screening workflows rather than a minor technical detail. Investment in appropriate query preparation strategies yields significant returns in improved hit rates, structural novelty, and overall screening success.
